Abdominal CT Protocol Overview

Abdominal CT anatomy illustration would appear here

Abdominal CT is used to evaluate the abdominal organs, vasculature, and gastrointestinal tract. Protocols vary based on clinical indication.

Common Indications

  • Abdominal pain
  • Oncology staging
  • Infection/inflammation
  • Trauma assessment
  • Renal stones

Technical Factors

  • 120 kVp
  • Auto mAs (noise index 12-15)
  • Slice thickness: 2.5-5mm
  • Pitch: 0.8-1.2
  • Rotation time: 0.5-0.8s

Contrast Administration

  • Oral contrast for bowel opacification
  • IV contrast for vascular enhancement
  • 100-120 mL contrast media
  • Injection rate: 2-3 mL/sec
  • Saline chaser: 30-40 mL

Standard Protocols

Protocol Slice Thickness Reconstruction Contrast Delay
Routine Abdomen/Pelvis 5mm / 2.5mm Soft Tissue, Bone IV ± Oral Portal venous (70s)
Triple Phase Liver 2.5mm Soft Tissue, MPR IV Arterial, Portal, Delayed
Renal Stone 2.5mm Soft Tissue, Bone None N/A
CT Enterography 2.5mm Soft Tissue, MPR IV + Neutral Oral Enteric (45-50s)

Patient Positioning for Abdominal CT

Patient positioning illustration would appear here

Standard Positioning

  • Supine position with arms raised above head
  • Center laser at level of xiphoid process
  • Use immobilization devices if needed
  • Ensure patient comfort to minimize motion
  • Use radiolucent padding for support

Special Positioning

  • Prone position for renal stone protocol
  • Left lateral decubitus for air contrast studies
  • Right side down for suspected appendicitis
  • Arms down for trauma patients if necessary

Breathing Instructions

Instruct patient to hold breath in inspiration during the scan. For longer acquisitions, use quiet shallow breathing technique.

Contrast Administration for Abdominal CT

Contrast administration illustration would appear here

Oral Contrast Protocols

  • Positive contrast: 800-1000mL 2% barium sulfate or iodinated contrast
  • Neutral contrast: Water or Volumen for CT enterography
  • Timing: 60-90 minutes prior to scan for small bowel
  • Emergency: 500-750mL given 20-30 minutes prior

IV Contrast Protocols

  • Standard: 100-120mL nonionic contrast (300-370 mgI/mL)
  • Injection rate: 2-3 mL/sec via power injector
  • Saline chaser: 30-40mL at same rate
  • Catheter size: 18-20 gauge preferred

Bolus Tracking

Place ROI on abdominal aorta at level of celiac axis. Trigger scan when attenuation reaches 100-150 HU.
