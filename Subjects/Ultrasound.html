What is Ultrasound?

Ultrasound imaging, also called sonography, is a medical imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to produce images of structures within the body. The images can provide valuable information for diagnosing and treating a variety of diseases and conditions.

Abdominal Ultrasound Protocol

Standard scanning protocol for comprehensive abdominal ultrasound examination:

  • Patient preparation: NPO for 6-8 hours
  • Use 2-5 MHz curvilinear transducer
  • Scan liver in sagittal, transverse, and subcostal views
  • Document all three liver lobes with measurements
  • Evaluate gallbladder in fasted state
  • Measure wall thickness and document any pathology
  • Visualize pancreas through acoustic windows
  • Document pancreas head, body, and tail
  • Scan both kidneys in longitudinal and transverse planes
  • Document renal length and parenchymal thickness
  • Evaluate spleen and document size
  • Scan abdominal aorta and document diameter
  • Evaluate inferior vena cava and patency

Common Abdominal Pathology

Frequently encountered pathological conditions in abdominal ultrasound:

Liver

  • Hepatic steatosis
  • Cirrhosis
  • Hepatomegaly
  • Liver masses
  • Hepatic cysts

Gallbladder

  • Cholelithiasis
  • Cholecystitis
  • Gallbladder polyps
  • Porcelain gallbladder
  • Sludge

Kidneys

  • Renal calculi
  • Hydronephrosis
  • Renal cysts
  • Medical renal disease
  • Masses

Pancreas

  • Pancreatitis
  • Pancreatic cysts
  • Ductal dilation
  • Pancreatic masses

Standard Measurement Guidelines

Normal reference ranges for abdominal organ measurements:

Organ Measurement Normal Range
Liver Length (midclavicular line) 10-15.5 cm
Gallbladder Wall thickness <3 mm
Common Bile Duct Inner diameter <6 mm
Pancreas Head AP diameter <3 cm
Kidney Length 9-12 cm
Spleen Length <13 cm
Aorta AP diameter <3 cm

Abdominal ultrasound evaluates the solid organs of the abdomen including the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, kidneys, and major blood vessels.

It is often the first imaging modality for abdominal symptoms.
